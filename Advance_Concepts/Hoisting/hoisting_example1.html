Hoisting: A problem with scatterd vars

Reference: JavaScript Patterns - Stoyan Stefanov

Chapter 2: Essentials

JavaScript enables you to have multiple var statements anywhere in a function, and they all act as if the variables were declared at the top of the function. This behavior is known as hoisting.

This can lead to logical errors when you use a variable and then you declare it further in the function. For JavaScript, as long as a variable is in the same scope (same function), it’s considered declared, even when it’s used before the var declaration.
